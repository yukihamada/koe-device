# Koe Seed Pricing Model (Bezos Edition)

## The Insight: Don't Sell Hardware. Sell Outcomes.

Festivals don't want 1000 tiny speakers. They want **"every attendee hears perfect audio."**
Price the outcome, not the box.

---

## Model A: Hardware Sale (Current — Elon's approach)
| Pack | Price | Revenue |
|------|-------|---------|
| 100 Seeds | $2,900 | One-time |
| 1,000 Seeds | $19,000 | One-time |
| 5,000 Seeds | $75,000 | One-time |

Problem: One-time revenue. No recurring. Customer pays once, uses forever.

---

## Model B: Koe Audio-as-a-Service (Bezos's approach)

### Subscription Tiers

| Tier | Seeds | Monthly | Annual | Includes |
|------|-------|---------|--------|----------|
| **Starter** | 100 | $290/mo | $2,900/yr | Seeds + Hub + Dashboard + OTA |
| **Pro** | 500 | $990/mo | $9,900/yr | + Priority support + Analytics |
| **Enterprise** | 2,000+ | Custom | Custom | + Dedicated CSM + API access |

### Per-Event Rental (Pay-as-you-go)
| Size | Seeds | Price/Event | Duration |
|------|-------|-------------|----------|
| Small (wedding, tour) | 50 | $200 | 1 day |
| Medium (corporate event) | 200 | $600 | 1 day |
| Large (festival) | 1,000 | $2,000 | 3 days |
| Stadium | 5,000 | $8,000 | 3 days |

**Ship a Pelican case of Seeds via FedEx. Customer returns after event.**

### The Math

1,000 Seeds cost $19,000 to manufacture.
Rent at $2,000/event × 20 events/year = **$40,000/year revenue**.
→ **2x ROI in year 1. Pure profit from year 2.**

Compare: Customer buys 1000 Seeds for $19,000 → we earn $19,000 total, ever.
Rental: We earn $40,000/year from the same 1000 Seeds → **$120,000 over 3 years.**

## Model C: Marketplace (the Amazon play)

We don't own all the Seeds. **Customers who bought Seeds rent them out when idle.**

### How it works
1. Festival A buys 1000 Seeds for their June event
2. Seeds sit idle July-May
3. Festival B needs 500 Seeds for their August event
4. Festival B rents 500 of Festival A's Seeds via Koe Marketplace
5. **Koe takes 20% commission** on the rental

### Revenue streams (Year 3)
| Stream | Revenue | Margin |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Hardware sales | $500K | 40% |
| Subscription (dashboard/analytics) | $300K | 90% |
| Rental marketplace commission | $200K | 95% |
| Data/analytics upsell | $100K | 95% |
| Sponsor audio insertion | $100K | 90% |
| **Total** | **$1.2M** | **~65%** |

---

## Recommended: Start with Model A, Evolve to B+C

### Phase 1 (Now → 6 months): Sell hardware (Model A)
- Simplest. Proves demand. Generates cash for manufacturing.
- Target: 5 festivals, $100K revenue.

### Phase 2 (6-12 months): Add subscription (Model B)
- Dashboard, OTA, analytics become the sticky product.
- Rental option for festivals that don't want to buy.
- Target: $30K MRR.

### Phase 3 (12-24 months): Marketplace (Model C)
- Network effects kick in. Every buyer becomes a supplier.
- Koe becomes the "Airbnb of audio equipment."
- Target: 50,000 Seeds in circulation, $100K/mo GMV.

---

## Bezos Decision: What to build FIRST

"If you can only build one thing this month, build the **dashboard**."

Why:
- Hardware is hardware. Anyone can copy it.
- The dashboard (fleet management, OTA, analytics) is the **lock-in**.
- Subscription revenue requires a dashboard.
- Marketplace requires a dashboard.
- Data monetization requires a dashboard.
- The dashboard is the platform. Seeds are just the edge devices.

This is the AWS lesson: the infrastructure is more valuable than the product it enables.

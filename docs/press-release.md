## A $19 Wristband Just Made $500,000 PA Systems Obsolete

**Tokyo, Japan — July 2026**

What if the entire audience *was* the sound system?

Koe today announced Koe Seed, a 28mm wireless audio receiver that straps to an attendee's wrist and replaces traditional PA speaker towers at festivals, concerts, and venues. For the first time, every single person in a crowd of 50,000 hears studio-quality audio — whether they're front row, back of the field, or in the bathroom line.

### The Problem

Festival audio is fundamentally broken. Front-row attendees get hearing damage at 110+ dB. Back-row attendees hear muffled echoes bouncing off buildings. Rain and wind destroy sound quality mid-set. PA system rentals cost $100,000-$500,000 per event. And noise complaints from neighbors limit venue options, operating hours, and artist bookings.

The industry has spent 50 years making speakers bigger. Koe made them disappear.

### The Solution

Event organizers hand out Koe Seeds at the gate — tiny Auracast receivers that slide into silicone wristbands. Attendees wear them. Each Seed receives audio via Bluetooth Auracast, a broadcast protocol that works like FM radio but digital. One transmitter, unlimited receivers. No WiFi, no apps, no pairing. Power on, music plays.

The wristband form factor changes everything: attendees never lose them (98% return rate vs 70% for loose devices), they survive rain and sweat (IP54), and distribution takes seconds at the gate.

## FAQ (Internal — Anticipated Customer Questions)

**Q: What if attendees steal the Seeds?**
A: At $19/unit, loss is budgeted. Wristband pods reduce loss to <2%. Deposit model ($10 refund on return) reduces to <1%. Even at 10% loss, the Festival Pack cost per event is $1,900 — still 80x cheaper than PA rental.

**Q: Why wristbands instead of just handing them out?**
A: Three reasons: (1) 98% return rate vs 70% for loose devices, saving $2,660/event on a 1,000-unit deployment. (2) Hands-free — attendees dance, eat, drink without holding anything. (3) Distribution is instant — slide into band at gate, collect at exit.

**Q: What about people who want to hear the live acoustic sound?**
A: Seeds have a volume dial (button press cycles volume). At outdoor festivals, the acoustic sound and Seed audio blend naturally. For indoor venues, Seeds can be the only audio source (silent disco mode).

**Q: Does this work with existing mixing desks?**
A: Yes. The Koe Hub connects to any mixer via XLR, TRS, or USB. It converts the audio to Auracast broadcast. No changes to the audio chain.

**Q: What about latency?**
A: 20-40ms. For live music, this is imperceptible. For lip-sync-critical applications (theater), a <10ms mode is available with Coded PHY disabled.

**Q: Can different zones have different audio?**
A: Yes. Each Hub broadcasts on a different Auracast stream. Seeds can be pre-assigned to zones, or attendees can switch channels with a button press.

**Q: Regulatory approval?**
A: Bluetooth 5.3 operates in the 2.4GHz ISM band, which is license-free worldwide. No special permits needed.
